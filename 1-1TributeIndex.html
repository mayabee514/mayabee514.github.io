Maurice Ralph Hilleman

Saved More Lives than Any Other Medical Scientist of the 20th Century

Dr. Maurice Ralph Hilleman in the lab


Timeline Of Dr. Hilleman's Life

  • 1919 - Born in Miles City, Montana

  • 1941 - Graduates his first class from Montana State University

  • 1944 - Receives doctoral degree in microbiology

  • 1957 - Discovers a new strain of flu that could potentially kill millions after an outbreak in Hong Kong. Disseminates vaccines that lead to less death due to this pandemic. Awarded the Distinguished Service Medal from the American Military. Joins Merck & Co. as Head of Research where he develops most of his 40 experimental and licensed animal and human vaccines

  • 1961 - Early pioneer warns of possible simian virus that contaminated the polio vaccine, which leads to a must more effective replacement oral vaccine

  • 1963 - Daughter Jeryl Lynn contracts the mumps. Cultivates material from her and uses it as a basis for the mumps vaccine still used today

  • 1981 - Licenses a vaccine for hepatitis B by treating blood with pepsin, urea and formaldehyde which is later withdrawn in 1986, to be replaced with a vaccine produced in yeast. His single greatest achievement was the development of this vaccine

  • 1984 - Retires at age 65

  • 1988 - President Ronald Reagan presents him with National Medal of Science

  • 2005 - dies in Philadelphia at the age of 85.
